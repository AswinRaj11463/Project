Facilitating Indian Airline Transportation: Export Assistance Strategies

Market Research and Intelligence:
  1. Indian airlines should conduct thorough market research to identify promising international markets with demand for air transportation services.
  2. Government agencies such as the Export-Import Bank of India (EXIM Bank) and the Ministry of Civil Aviation can provide valuable market intelligence, including data on air travel trends, competitor analysis, and regulatory requirements in target markets.
Trade Promotion Programs:
  1. Indian airlines can leverage trade promotion programs organized by government bodies like the Federation of Indian Chambers of Commerce and Industry (FICCI) and the Confederation of Indian Industry (CII) to showcase their services, establish contacts with potential buyers, and explore partnership opportunities abroad.

Current Trade Barriers:

  1. Bilateral Air Service Agreements (BASAs): Indian airlines often face restrictions imposed by BASAs, which regulate air traffic rights between India and other countries. These agreements may limit the number of flights, routes, and capacity, hindering the expansion of Indian carriers into lucrative international markets.

  2. Foreign Ownership Restrictions: Indian regulations restrict foreign ownership in domestic airlines to 49%. This limitation impedes foreign investment and collaboration, limiting the capital infusion and strategic partnerships that could enhance the competitiveness of Indian carriers.

  3. High Aviation Taxes and Charges: Indian airlines grapple with high aviation taxes, fuel surcharges, and airport charges, which increase operational costs and ticket prices. These excessive charges diminish the affordability of air travel and inhibit the growth of the aviation industry.

Media Inquiries

  1. For Indian airline media inquiries, journalists and media professionals typically reach out to the respective airline's corporate communications or media relations department. These departments serve as the primary point of contact for all media-related queries, including requests for interviews, statements, press releases, and information on company developments or events. Indian airlines understand the importance of transparency and timely communication with the media to address public inquiries, provide updates on operational matters, and manage the airline's reputation effectively. Additionally, airlines often have dedicated media relations teams tasked with managing media inquiries, cultivating relationships with journalists, and coordinating press activities to ensure accurate and timely coverage of the airline's initiatives and achievements in the media landscape.
